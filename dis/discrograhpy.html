On August 18, fans discovered through an Apple Music album preview that Daniel would be featuring in “Hush Hush”, the 6th track from MIYAVI’s 13th studio album Imaginary released on September 15. The album was originally planned shortly after his April 2020 album Holy Nights, but he postponed to 2021 due to recent pandemic developments and because the content he wished to express changed. Special Kollection virtual items inspired by the collaboration’s music video would be launched.[s] An audio teaser was shared on September 13.[s]

Daniel revealed in his August 29 V LIVE that MIYAVI reached out to Daniel when he was shooting the “Antidote” music video with two proposed collaboration songs. Daniel liked both but picked the track he thought he could do better in and recorded the song in June.[s] MIYAVI said in Spice and MeMeOn interviews he felt Daniel was now establishing his style as an artist and trying to be mature. Daniel’s staff also knew of MIYAVI and listened to him for a long time, so the collaboration proceeded quickly. MIYAVI said he met Daniel through the staff and thinks Daniel is a very popular artist who is serious and straightforward too. He felt that there are many discoveries for him and Daniel through this song. According to TV LIFE, MIYAVI listened to Daniel’s work and felt it changed recently and MIYAVI also felt he himself had a settled direction or awakening as an artist. There was another song candidate but “Hush Hush” received good feedback, so MIYAVI decided to include it in the album.[s] [s] [s]

In a September 1 OKMUsic interview, MIYAVI said many genres make up his style and that Kang Daniel contributed with wonderful singing.[s] In a September 14 ananWEB interview, MIYAVI shared that the collaboration was produced remotely due to COVID-19. Even if they hadn’t met, it was fate that they were able to connect and complete it. Daniel helped express things that MIYAVI couldn’t express by himself alone and expanded the world view of the song, so MIYAVI was very happy. [s] A Natalie interviewer remarked “Hush Hush” has a slightly boy band-like taste that is rare for MIYAVI. MIYAVI said he sang about the contradiction between freedom and restraint and the anger in there. Daniel’s neutral voice and MIYAVI’s voice are mixed to create a mysterious sense of detune.[s]

Daniel told Barks, “It was an honor to work with MIYAVI on “Hush Hush”. The song was very meaningful to work on because it represents those without a voice. MIYAVI is a legend in his craft and such a visionary artist. The entire creative process was inspiring and, truly, a dream come true.” [s]
